How strategies are classified

  • Water — beats Buy & Hold 1× on CAGR and/or max drawdown while not sacrificing any metric (Sharpe, Sortino, Calmar, MaxDD, volatility all no worse; at least one of CAGR or MaxDD strictly better).
  • Octane — strictly higher CAGR and Calmar than Buy & Hold 1×, with max drawdown no worse than −45% and at most 30 trades per year. (Water takes precedence when both qualify.)
  • Stillwater (Nasdaq 100 only) — the Water/Octane risk criteria, but CAGR may be up to 2 percentage points below Buy & Hold 1×. Surfaces defensive variants where strict Water/Octane is unreachable because the benchmark's high CAGR comes only with an extreme (−82%) drawdown.

★ marks the strategy this site features as its default / chosen pick for that asset (S&P Octane & Water tabs; Nasdaq Stillwater). Other assets' pages run the Guarded A5/B25 strategy, which is outside this Excel sweep universe.
